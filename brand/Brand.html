Visual Identity

Logo design and visual elements that represent the brand

Logo Design System

Symbol Structure

The Katalyst logo features a molecular-like connection structure integrated with the "K" letterform, representing interconnected processes, systematic methodology, and the catalyst effect of transformation. The three circular nodes connected by angular lines symbolize:

  • Three service areas: Agile PM, Health & Wellness, Education
  • Molecular catalyst structure enabling transformation
  • Connected systems and collaborative processes
  • Agile iteration cycles and continuous improvement
Logo Variations

Blue Version (#344877): Professional contexts, corporate applications, official documentation

Orange Version (#EEA437): Marketing materials, call-to-action elements, energetic applications

Symbol Only: Favicons, social media profiles, compact applications

Technical Specifications

Minimum size: 24px digital / 0.5" print | Clear space: One node height | Formats: SVG, PNG, EPS

Brand Guidelines Summary

Essential rules and standards for consistent brand implementation

Essential Brand Guidelines

Visual Identity

Use molecular "K" logo with proper spacing. Primary: Blue (#344877), Secondary: Orange (#EEA437)

Core Message

"Agile Project Management for Any Industry" - emphasize universal applicability with specialized expertise

Typography

Headlines: Montserrat Bold, Body: Montserrat Regular, Taglines: Comfortaa

Brand Voice

Authoritative + approachable, data-driven, collaborative, results-focused

Evidence Requirement

Always support claims with specific data, metrics, or examples

Methodology Focus

Emphasize four-phase systematic approach and cross-industry expertise

✓ Always Ensure
  • Logo properly sized and positioned
  • Colors match brand specifications
  • Typography follows hierarchy
  • Claims supported by data
  • Cross-industry message clear
  • Professional yet approachable tone
✗ Never Allow
  • Generic consulting language
  • Unsupported claims or promises
  • Logo distortion or wrong colors
  • Industry-specific only messaging
  • Overly technical or casual tone
  • Missing measurement focus
